Background

Angiopoietin-2 (Ang2) is one of the principal growth factors involved in angiogenesis, differing from Angiopoietin-1 (Ang1) in that it primarily functions via autocrine signaling and does not bind with the Tie1 receptor. Under physiological conditions, Ang2 inhibits the Tie2 phosphorylation induced by Ang1, and phosphorylates Tie2 at a lower intensity, thereby inducing endothelial cell function and promoting lymphatic growth. Additionally, research suggests that Ang2 functions via integrins as alternative receptors.

Furthermore, Ang2 plays a central role in diseases related to vascular permeability and angiogenesis. During inflammatory conditions or tumor progression, upregulated Ang2 expression increases the permeability of endothelial cells, induces pericyte detachment from the basal lamina, which leads to vascular leakage, and promotes tumor proliferation and metastasis, consequently exacerbating disease conditions.
